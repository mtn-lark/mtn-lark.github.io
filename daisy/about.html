I adopted Daisy in February 2023. I was looking to adopt a cat as an emotional support animal, and overheard that a friend of a friend was taking care of a street cat near campus and was looking for someone to adopt it.

I got her contact info and quickly started visiting the alley that Daisy lived in. Even as a street cat, he was beautiful and pretty friendly! However, he was very large and did NOT like to be picked up, so it took me visiting the alley 5 times with a carrier (and the help of my friend's mom) to finally take him to a vet and then home. At the vet, Daisy got vaccinations and a microchip. He was so sweet, they couldn't believe I had picked him up off the street!

After the vet, I took Daisy home. Even though he was in a totally new environment, he was happy to explore and find new places to hide. At first, he just slept most of the time, occasionally coming out to find more places to explore and sit next to me. It was less than a month before he was sleeping with me at night, trying to sit on my laptop, and running around chasing toys.

Unfortunately, after many months or years on the street, Daisy was not in the best shape. Over the next year, Daisy had to endure a weight-loss diet before finally reaching a healthy weight of 13 pounds. He had a broken tooth pulled so he wouldn't be in pain. Having never owned a cat before, I had a lot of learning to do, too. I had to learn how to give him medicine and clip his nails, two things he still doesn't like.

Today, Daisy loves to lounge on our dorm couch and chase spring toys. He is as friendly and fluffy as the day I got him. He has learned to love sitting on laps and making biscuits, and to tolerate having his majestic coat brushed. He tries to eat any yarn I use to crochet. He has learned a grand total of 1 trick: standing on his back legs to get a treat! Daisy can still be pretty jumpy -- he hides when someone knocks on our door, and has to take anti-anxiety medicine for car rides to the vet. But overall, he is the perfect cat for me :)
